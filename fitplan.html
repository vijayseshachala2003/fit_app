☀️ Morning routine — evidence highlights

Morning light, hydration, and timed movement help align circadian rhythms and alertness. Regular exercise improves sleep and health regardless of clock time; when afternoons are blocked by work, morning sessions avoid late-night high strain that can disturb sleep.

06:15 · Wake
Fixed wake time
Wake at the same time daily (including most weekends); large shifts (“social jet lag”) disrupt rhythm.
National Heart, Lung, and Blood Institute — consistent sleep schedule.
06:15–06:35 · Light + water
Outdoor daylight & fluid
Within ~30 min of waking, get natural outdoor light (even cloudy sky dwarfs typical indoor lux). Drink 300–500 ml water — overnight fluid loss contributes to grogginess.
Circadian timing reviews; mild dehydration impairs mood and cognition in trials.
06:35–06:55 · Optional fuel
Small snack if fasted
Banana, dates, or toast — optional if you train better fed. Heavy sits poorly right before hard sets.
Personal tolerance; align with your Meals tab.
07:00–08:15 · Gym
Strength session (≤65 min)
Warm up 10+ min (morning core temp is lower). Matches your FitPlan timer cap. RCT data in sedentary adults found morning aerobic training advanced melatonin rhythm and shortened sleep latency vs baseline; evening had different vascular perks — both exercise groups benefited.
12-week RCT — morning vs evening aerobic exercise & sleep (Scientific Reports, 2025).
08:15–09:30 · Refuel
Shower + protein-rich breakfast
Paneer, Greek yogurt, dal — hit recovery targets from your meal plan. Breakfast closes glycogen loop after training.
Breakfast consumption linked to higher micronutrient intake in observational data.

🌙 Evening routine — sleep-first

Large wearable cohort study: later exercise + higher cardiovascular strain associates with delayed sleep, shorter sleep, lower quality, and higher night resting HR. Bouts ending ≥4 hours before sleep onset were not linked to those disruptions — relevant because your hard training is mornings.

20:00 · Leave office
Hard stop → commute home
Ease stimulant use after mid-afternoon if sleep is fragile — caffeine can linger hours (individual sensitivity varies).
NHLBI notes caffeine/nicotine stimulant effects on sleep.
20:30–21:15 · Dinner
Main meal, lighter if late
Avoid very heavy/spicy loads close to bedtime; light snack OK if hungry.
NHLBI healthy sleep habits; Sleep Foundation bedtime routine guidance.
21:15–21:45 · Recovery only
Walk, mobility, breath — not HIIT
If anything extra: easy movement. Save intensity for morning — matches strain-timing findings.
Nature Communications (2025) evening strain vs sleep in free-living adults.
21:45 · Wind-down alarm
Start 60–90 min pre-bed routine
Shift to warm dim lights; put work away. CDC/NIOSH suggests preparing for sleep ~90 min ahead.
CDC NIOSH sleep bulletin; NHLBI “quiet hour” before bed.
21:45–22:45 · Analog recovery
Shower, stretch, paper book / audio
Reduce bright screens — blue-enriched light suppresses melatonin; Night Shift + low brightness helps but doesn’t fully replace dim ambient light.
Sleep hygiene reviews; Sleep Foundation screen guidance.
22:45–23:15 · Bedroom
Cool, dark, quiet
Target 7–9 hours for adults — if wake ~06:15, aim lights-out ~21:15–23:45 depending on need.
CDC & NIH adult sleep duration recommendations.
